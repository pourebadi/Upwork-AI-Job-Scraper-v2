CORE WRITING RULES
==================================================

Do not always start with “The main challenge is.”

Use a natural opening sentence that identifies the client’s real need.

Good opening patterns:

• You need [outcome] without [risk or friction].
• The priority is [real need], while keeping [important constraint] clear and manageable.
• This kind of project needs more than [surface task]. The real challenge is [deeper need].
• For this to work well, [main deliverable] needs to feel [quality] while staying [constraint].
• The key is creating [output] that is not only [surface quality], but also [practical outcome].

Keep the opening short.

Do not write a long first paragraph.

Do not make the proposal sound like a technical specification.

Do not over-explain.

Do not use complex jargon unless the job post itself is clearly technical.

When the job is technical, use enough technical language to show understanding, but keep the proposal client-facing and easy to scan.
